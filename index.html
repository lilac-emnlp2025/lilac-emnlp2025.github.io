Personalizing Vision-Language-Action Models with Visual Attentive Prompting

Sangoh Lee, Sangwoo Mo, Wook-Shin Han
Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH)
Paper arXiv Video Code

VAP enables frozen VLA models to manipulate user-specific objects (e.g., "my cup") among visually similar distractors without any training.

Abstract

While Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models generalize well to generic instructions, they struggle with personalized commands such as "bring my cup," where the robot must act on one specific instance among visually similar objects.

To address this, we propose Visual Attentive Prompting (VAP), a simple-yet-effective training-free perceptual adapter. VAP equips frozen VLAs with top-down selective attention by grounding the user-specific object from a few reference images and injecting this knowledge as a visual prompt (e.g., highlighting the object and rewriting the instruction).

Our extensive experiments on Personalized-SIMPLER, Personalized-VLABench, and real-world setups show that VAP significantly outperforms generic policies and token-learning baselines.
